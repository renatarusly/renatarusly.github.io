Case study • Product concept

ACADEME

A research-driven experience to help students and parents navigate school/university selection with clarity, credible information, and less overwhelm.

← Back to works

Overview

Role
UI/UX Product Designer
Methods
Survey research • Prototyping • Testing • Pitching
Context
Final project (Generation Girl UI/UX Bootcamp)

Problem

Prospective students and parents face scattered, hard-to-verify information across rankings, listings, and university websites. Comparing options becomes stressful, time-consuming, and difficult to trust.

Research highlights

After sharing a survey with 50+ respondents, I synthesized themes around what matters most when selecting a school/university:

  • Clarity: users want concise, organized information instead of cluttered pages.
  • Facilities & environment: a major factor in evaluating fit and quality of life.
  • Scholarships: many respondents actively search for financial support signals.
  • Rankings: important for many, but not all; needs to be presented transparently.

Design decisions (tied to user needs)

Insight → Decision

Users feel overwhelmed by too many sources → structured listings that combine key data points in one place.

Insight → Decision

Trust matters for parents → emphasize credibility by clearly labeling information sources and rankings.

Insight → Decision

Students want personalized fit → guided questions to filter by location, budget, major, and preferences.

UX pattern

Make comparing easy → comparison-first layout and “favorites” to shortlist and revisit candidates.

Results & takeaways

This project taught me how research shapes product direction—from identifying user pain points to defining features like credible listings, guided search, and comparison tools.

  • Clarity is key: information must be concise, credible, and easy to navigate.
  • Different users, different needs: parents value trust; students value personalization.
  • Research drives design: insights shaped structure and flow, not just visuals.

Reflection & future improvements

  • Broader credibility: integrate data from multiple trusted sources and ranking systems.
  • Simpler flow: reduce steps and visual clutter to improve speed and scan-ability.
  • Better personalization: tailor results based on goals, interests, and career direction.
