About Me

Born and raised in Jakarta, Indonesia 🇮🇩, now studying Computer Science with Management in London 🇬🇧. I’ve always been fascinated by how things look and work — from doodling as a kid to designing interfaces that feel simple and human.

Through my projects, I’ve learned that people are endlessly fascinating — every behavior, hesitation, and smile teaches something new. I’m currently seeking opportunities in UI/UX and product design to keep learning, creating, and growing.

01 • Product Design

MOOD2GO

Problem: People often struggle to decide where to go, leading to repetitive outings and decision fatigue.

Design decision: A mood + vibe + radius flow that turns indecision into quick, playful discovery.

Why: Observation showed groups spending 15–20 minutes debating; a guided flow reduces time-to-decision.

ACADEME

Problem: Students and parents face overwhelming, scattered information when choosing universities.

Design decision: A comparison-first structure that surfaces credible information, scholarships, and facilities.

Why: Research indicated users value clarity and trust; structured listings reduce cognitive load and uncertainty.

03 • Platform Design

SIKKUT

Problem: Employee wellness programs were fragmented and hard to navigate, reducing engagement.

Design decision: A unified portal that integrates programs under one navigation system and consistent UI.

Why: Fragmented visuals increased confusion; a shared system improves recognition and speeds navigation.
