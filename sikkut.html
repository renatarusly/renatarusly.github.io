Case study • Web platform

SIKKUT

A unified employee wellness portal for United Tractors—integrating multiple programs into one organized, consistent experience. (Innovactive Competition: 3rd place among 100+ groups)

← Back to works

Overview

Role
UI Designer • Branding
Focus
Interaction design • Visual design • Prototyping • Testing
Constraint
Strict brand guidelines (black/yellow corporate palette)

Problem

United Tractors offers multiple wellness programs (e.g., clinic, Selaras), but they were managed separately—making it hard for employees to find services quickly, trust what they see, and move through tasks without confusion.

Objective

Establish a unified visual system and information structure that integrates existing programs into one clear portal—reducing cognitive load while keeping the experience professional and on-brand.

Design decisions (tied to user behavior)

I focused on decisions that reduce friction and improve recognition across programs, while preserving the corporate identity.

Observation → Decision

Users often tap outside modals to close them (observed in 8/10 internal test users) → support click-outside close to keep flows simple.

Recognition → Decision

Unified home with key services → display core programs in one place to reduce navigation time.

Clarity → Decision

Human icons instead of numeric capacity → availability is read faster and feels more intuitive at a glance.

Speed → Decision

Half-and-half layout → users can choose a facility and complete a form without switching screens.

Trust → Decision

High contrast + consistent typography → improves readability and professional credibility across the portal.

Results & takeaways

The redesign centralized eight key services under one clear navigation system. Internal testing showed faster navigation and improved recognition across programs through consistent visuals.

  • Unified experience: multiple wellness programs in one cohesive platform.
  • Improved usability: simpler access for employees across levels/divisions.
  • Accessible by design: better contrast and readability while preserving brand identity.

Reflection & future improvements

Designing SIKKUT taught me how to balance corporate identity with usability—and how consistency can improve trust at scale.

  • Mobile accessibility: make wellness access easier on-the-go.
  • Personalized dashboards: tailored to employee habits and frequent actions.
  • Analytics: help HR measure engagement and program impact.
